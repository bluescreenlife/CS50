sort1 uses: BUBBLE SORT

How do you know?: Performs faster when numbers are already in sorted order.

sort2 uses: MERGE SORT 

How do you know?: Fastest with large randomized data sets, due to recursive, split-effort nature.

sort3 uses: SELECTION SORT

How do you know?: Slower with numbers already in sorted order, as it has to check each value
against the rest of the values with each pass.
